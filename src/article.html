When a medicine shortage occurs, patients — our loved ones — are the ones most affected.
Parallel distribution can help fill the gap. Here’s more on the impact it has on all of us:

Why shortages exist & how we can solve them

That medicine shortages occur in Europe is an unfortunate fact. But it’s not a reality
we must accept. Here are the main problems that cause shortages:

Manufacturing problems

When a
factory temporarely closes
or production lag times occur,
medicine shortages take place.

Supply chain problems

Quotas, inefficiency in logistics,
and relying on too few suppliers lead to medicine shortages.

Regulatory problems

Lag time, national requirements,
and the lack of harmonisation lead to medicine shortages.

How
it works:

Parallel distribution is simple. When a company purchases medicine in one EU country and sells it in another, all the while complying with the highest quality standards, that’s parallel distribution.

Benefits:

When a country experiences a medicine shortage, parallel distribution fills the gap. How? Say Belgium has a shortage. Parallel distributors fill the gap by taking surplus from other EU countries and delivering it to Belgium. Without this process, patients miss out on the medications they need to live a healthy, happy life.
